A Tribute To Dr. Norman Borlaug

1914 - 2009
A Man That Fed Millions

A photo taken of Dr. Borlaug on September 1, 2009


This page is a tribute to the brilliant scientist and philanthropist, Dr. Norman Borlaug. Borlaug was born in Cresco, Iowa, on a farm and all of his work started in 1944, when he particpipated in the Rockefeller Foundation's pioneering technical assistance program in Mexico. He is regarded as the man who saved millions of lives due to his discoveries and persistance in the field of plant pathology and creating easier methods to grow crops.
